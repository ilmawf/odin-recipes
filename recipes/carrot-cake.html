Carrot Cake

Carrot cake is a moist and flavorful dessert that combines the natural sweetness of carrots with warm spices, creating a comforting and delicious treat. The base of the cake is made from finely grated carrots, which add a subtle sweetness and a tender texture. The batter is enriched with spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves, and often includes ingredients such as crushed walnuts or pecans for added crunch. The cake is typically baked until golden brown and then cooled before being frosted.

A classic cream cheese frosting is often used to complement the rich flavors of the carrot cake. The tangy cream cheese provides a perfect balance to the sweetness of the cake, creating a luscious and smooth finish. Garnished with additional nuts or a sprinkle of cinnamon, this cake is a popular choice for celebrations and gatherings, offering a delightful mix of flavors and textures in every bite.

Ingredients

Cake:
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 1 ¼ cups vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
Frosting
  • ½ cup butter, softened
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 4 cups confectioners' sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour a 9x13-inch pan.
  2. Beat sugar, oil, eggs, and 2 teaspoons vanilla together in a large bowl with an electric mixer until well combined. Mix in flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Stir in carrots. Fold in pecans. Pour into the prepared pan.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean, about 40 minutes. Let cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack and cool completely.
  4. To make the frosting: Beat butter, cream cheese, confectioners' sugar, and 1 teaspoon vanilla together in a large bowl with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy. Stir in chopped pecans. Frost the cooled cake.
